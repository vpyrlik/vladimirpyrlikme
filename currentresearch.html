Current Research

Shrinkage for Gaussian and t copulas in ultra-high dimensions
Jointly with S. Anatolyev
CERGE-EI Working paper series (2021)
Highlights:
  • Methods of covariance matrix shrinkage are applied to estimate parameters of Gaussian and t copulas in ultra-high dimensions.
  • Simulations illustrate dominance of shrinkage estimators over traditional copula estimators.
  • The approach is applied for a large portfolio allocation problem with up to 3600 assets.

Two-step estimation of high-dimensional skew-t copula
Work in progress
Highlights:
  • A novel approach to skew-t copula estimation based on method of moments and shrinkage is suggested.
  • The estimator is practical and well-behaved under either low or high dimensionality.
  • The suggested approach is applied to improve portfolio allocation under potential tail dependency of the assets returns.
Machine learning and mixed-frequency data based forecasting of Realized volatility (the case of Russian Stock Market)
Jointly with P. Elizarov and A. Leonova
Work in progress
Highlights:
  • A variety of machine learning algorithms are studied in the task of assets returns' realized volatility forecasting in comparison with the benchmark HAR-RV framework
  • To boost-up the predictive power of the models, numerous mixed-frequency data on the assets, their market or related markets, state or world economy is included.
  • Lasso regressions generally outperform other predictors, with valuable information about the future volatility dynamics provided by the indicators of the economies and markets.
